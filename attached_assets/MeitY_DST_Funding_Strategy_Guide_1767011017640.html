1.2 MeitY Cyber Security R&D Program ‚Äî Deep Dive

Program Overview

The Cyber Security R&D program is administered by MeitY's Cyber Security Division and aims to develop indigenous cybersecurity solutions. This is the most relevant program for T-SPAN given our focus on DNS threat intelligence and sovereign security.

Thrust Areas (2024-2027)

  • Threat Intelligence Platforms ‚Äî Real-time threat detection, IOC sharing, STIX/TAXII integration
  • AI/ML for Cybersecurity ‚Äî Anomaly detection, malware classification, behavioral analysis
  • DNS Security ‚Äî DNSSEC, DNS-over-HTTPS, DNS-based threat blocking
  • Network Security ‚Äî DDoS mitigation, intrusion detection, traffic analysis
  • Critical Infrastructure Protection ‚Äî SCADA/ICS security, power grid, financial systems
  • Cryptographic Solutions ‚Äî Post-quantum cryptography, homomorphic encryption
  • Security Operations Centers ‚Äî SOC automation, SIEM development, incident response
T-SPAN's ATLANTIS AI engine and SIEP protocol align perfectly with MeitY's thrust areas on "Threat Intelligence Platforms" and "AI/ML for Cybersecurity." Frame proposals around sovereign, indigenous solutions to maximize alignment.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Indian academic institution (AICTE/UGC recognized) ‚Äî MRDU qualifies
  • R&D organization registered in India
  • Industry partner with 51%+ Indian ownership (for joint projects)
  • Principal Investigator must be Indian citizen with PhD/equivalent
  • Institution must have existing research infrastructure
  • No pending utilization certificates from previous grants

Funding Components Allowed

1.3 Application Process ‚Äî MeitY Cyber Security R&D

1
Expression of Interest (EOI)

Submit 2-3 page concept note through MeitY portal when call is announced. Include problem statement, proposed approach, expected outcomes, and team credentials. Timeline: 2-4 weeks from call announcement.

2
Full Proposal Submission

If EOI shortlisted, submit detailed proposal (15-25 pages) including technical approach, methodology, work packages, deliverables, timeline, budget, and team CVs. Use MeitY prescribed format. Timeline: 4-6 weeks.

3
Expert Committee Evaluation

Technical proposal reviewed by domain experts. May require presentation to committee (in-person at Delhi or virtual). Prepare demo of existing work. Timeline: 2-3 months.

4
Budget Negotiation

If technically approved, budget scrutinized by finance division. Be prepared for 15-30% cuts. Justify each line item with quotations. Timeline: 1-2 months.

5
Sanction & MoU

Formal sanction letter issued. Sign MoU with MeitY specifying deliverables, timelines, IP ownership, and reporting requirements. Timeline: 1 month.

6
Fund Release

First installment (typically 50%) released after MoU signing. Subsequent releases based on milestone completion and utilization certificate submission. Timeline: 2-4 weeks after MoU.

‚è±Ô∏è Total Timeline: 8-14 months from call to fund release

Plan applications 12-18 months before funds are needed. MeitY typically announces 2-3 calls per year in cybersecurity.

1.4 Multiplier Grants Scheme (MGS) ‚Äî Industry-Academia

Why MGS is Perfect for T-SPAN

The Multiplier Grants Scheme provides matching funds for industry-sponsored academic research. If TCPWave commits ‚Çπ1 Crore to MRDU research, MeitY will match with ‚Çπ1 Crore (up to ‚Çπ2 Crore total from government). This is the fastest and most flexible MeitY funding mechanism.

MGS Structure

Industry Contribution MeitY Matching Total Project Size
‚Çπ25 Lakhs ‚Çπ25 Lakhs (1:1) ‚Çπ50 Lakhs
‚Çπ50 Lakhs ‚Çπ50 Lakhs (1:1) ‚Çπ1 Crore
‚Çπ1 Crore ‚Çπ1 Crore (1:1) ‚Çπ2 Crores
‚Çπ2 Crores+ ‚Çπ2 Crores (capped) ‚Çπ4 Crores+
Structure TCPWave's R&D commitment to MRDU as MGS-eligible industry sponsorship. This doubles the available research budget at no additional cost to TCPWave.

MGS Application Requirements

  • Joint proposal from industry (TCPWave) and academia (MRDU)
  • Industry partner must commit funds in escrow before MeitY approval
  • Research must address MeitY priority areas
  • IP sharing agreement must be pre-defined (typically 50:50 or as negotiated)
  • Industry partner must be registered in India (TCPWave India subsidiary qualifies)

2.2 SERB Core Research Grant (CRG) ‚Äî Deep Dive

Best Fit for Foundational Research

The CRG scheme funds investigator-driven research proposals. This is ideal for the theoretical foundations of T-SPAN ‚Äî proving the Gadi-Chinnam Bound, developing SIEP protocol specifications, and publishing in top-tier venues.

CRG Eligibility

  • Principal Investigator must hold PhD and regular faculty position
  • Age limit: Below 60 years at time of submission
  • PI cannot hold more than 2 ongoing SERB projects
  • Institution must be recognized academic/research organization
  • No pending project completion reports from PI

CRG Application Process

1
Online Registration

Register on SERB online portal (serbonline.in). Create account, verify institution details, and link ORCID/Scopus IDs.

2
Proposal Preparation

Prepare proposal following SERB format: Summary, Introduction, Objectives, Methodology, Work Plan, Expected Outcomes, Budget Justification, and PI CV. 15-20 pages max.

3
Online Submission

Submit through SERB portal. System generates unique proposal ID. Download submission receipt. Calls are typically open year-round; rolling review.

4
Expert Review

Proposal sent to 3 external reviewers (blind). Based on reviews, may be: Recommended, Recommended with Revision, or Not Recommended. Timeline: 3-6 months.

5
Programme Advisory Committee (PAC)

If reviewer-recommended, proposal goes to PAC for final decision. PI may be called for presentation. Timeline: 2-3 months.

6
Sanction & Fund Release

Sanction letter issued. Institution signs bond. First installment (Year 1 + 50% equipment) released. Timeline: 1-2 months.

Part IV: Proposal Writing Strategy

4.1 The Winning Proposal Structure

  • Executive Summary (1 page) ‚Äî Problem, approach, outcomes, budget in plain language
  • Problem Statement (2 pages) ‚Äî Why this matters nationally, current gaps, urgency
  • Literature Review (2 pages) ‚Äî What exists, why it's insufficient, your differentiation
  • Objectives (1 page) ‚Äî 3-5 specific, measurable objectives
  • Methodology (4-5 pages) ‚Äî Technical approach, algorithms, architectures, datasets
  • Work Plan (2 pages) ‚Äî Gantt chart, milestones, deliverables by quarter
  • Expected Outcomes (1 page) ‚Äî Publications, patents, prototypes, deployments
  • Team & Facilities (2 pages) ‚Äî PI/Co-PI CVs, existing infrastructure, institutional support
  • Budget Justification (2 pages) ‚Äî Line-item justification with quotations
  • References (1-2 pages) ‚Äî Cite recent, relevant literature

4.2 Magic Words That Reviewers Love

Phrase Signals Use In

4.3 Common Rejection Reasons & How to Avoid

Rejection Reason Frequency Avoidance Strategy
Vague objectives 35% Use SMART criteria: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
Insufficient preliminary work 25% Include proof-of-concept results, even if limited. Show you've started.
Weak team qualifications 15% Add co-PIs from IITs/IISc if needed. List relevant publications prominently.
Unjustified budget 10% Attach vendor quotations. Explain each line item's necessity.
Not aligned with call 10% Mirror the call language. Use exact thrust area terminology.
Poor presentation 5% Professional formatting, clear figures, no typos. Have others review.

Part VII: Advanced Strategies

7.1 Building Relationships Before Applying

Don't submit proposals cold. Schedule meetings with Program Directors 3-6 months before application. Understand their priorities. Get feedback on concept notes. This dramatically improves success rates.

Relationship Building Actions

  • Attend MeitY/DST workshops and conferences
  • Invite Program Directors to T-SPAN events
  • Offer to review proposals for the ministry (builds credibility)
  • Publish op-eds on cybersecurity in national media (visibility)
  • Brief ministry officials on T-SPAN progress (keep them informed)

7.2 Leveraging DSCI CCoE Connection

DSCI as Force Multiplier

DSCI (Data Security Council of India) has strong relationships with MeitY and industry. Use DSCI CCoE connection to:

  • Get introductions to MeitY officials
  • Participate in DSCI-organized ministry consultations
  • Co-brand proposals as "DSCI CCoE supported"
  • Access DSCI industry network for MGS partnerships
